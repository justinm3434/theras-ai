Our Thesis

Training happens once.
Inference happens forever.

Today's AI infrastructure runs inference on hardware designed for training — general-purpose chips that spend most of their energy just moving data around. But once a model is trained, its core parameters converge. The fundamental representations stabilize. The weights don't change.

TherasAI is building inference hardware that exploits this insight. We encode stable model parameters directly into the metal interconnect layers of the chip — eliminating the memory wall entirely. No DRAM reads. No bandwidth bottleneck. Weights become part of the chip itself.

The result is inference that's orders of magnitude faster and more energy-efficient — at a fraction of the cost per token.

01

Parameters in Metal

Core model weights are physically encoded in the final metal layers during fabrication. The chip doesn't read the weights — it is the weights.

02

Weeks, Not Years

Base dies are pre-fabricated in bulk. Only the final metal layers are customized per model — taking the timeline from 18 months to weeks.

03

The 95% Problem

Training is 5% of total AI compute. Inference is the other 95% — and it's running on hardware that wasn't built for it. We're building hardware that is.
